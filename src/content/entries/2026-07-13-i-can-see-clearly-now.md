Today was a BIG DAY! It was the "unveiling of the foot!" Aka, the day all the surgical dressing came off and I finally got to lay eyes on the masterpiece that [Dr. Stein](/entries/2026-06-17-doctors-orders) forged. And it was *glorious*.

Now, heads up here... there are going to be some squeamish photos, so if you don't want to see the work in progress, do not scroll any further. You've been warned. We'll start with the unwrapping. So much padding and wrapping goes into keeping this thing protected the first two weeks! It's like a giant surgical pillow wrapped around my entire foot and ankle. TBH I didn't really mind it, although it did get hot at times. You can see why with all this padding!

Ok, now we can get back to the story. This is where I am now:

<img src="/entries/foot-final.jpeg" alt="The after picture: my right foot and lower leg freshly rewrapped in tan compression bandage, a white DARCO toe alignment splint strapped around the forefoot holding my big toe straight, a few bruised toes and the pin tip peeking out, with my left foot in its white-and-orange HOKA nearby." />

Basically, everything looked *amazing* and is really healing well. But if you saw the photos above, you know there are a LOT of stitches. And there are two sets on the top and inside of my foot that are pulling the muscles and tendons below the skin in opposite directions. Since there are so many incisions, they thought it best to let it all go one more week before removing the stitches, just so nothing accidentally reopens. And honestly, I'm fine with that. Nothing hurts. Why tempt fate, right?! So I'll return next Monday for all of those to come out. In the meantime, as you can see above, the foot has been rewrapped and I'm wearing a [toe spacer/sling](https://www.darcointernational.com/toe-alignment-splint) that holds the big toe straight, again, to provide some support and help with healing alignment. We'll also hold off on x-rays until 6 weeks, so that [nifty set of hardware](/entries/2026-06-15-some-assembly-required) will continue to remain elusive for the time being.

So today's win was dressing off, shiny new heavy metal boot on, with the goal of not snagging [that pin coming out of my second toe](/entries/2026-07-10-under-pressure) on anything. Oh, and that will come out at 6 weeks as well, so lots to look forward to next month 🙂

Onward and upward.
