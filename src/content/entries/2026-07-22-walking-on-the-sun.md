I've been putting this off for days. But it's time to name the elephant in the room, the one thing in my running life I've never been able to quit.

Badwater.

It shouldn't surprise anyone; I've name-dropped it here more than once. Every year, right around the third week of July, I slip into a strange little place. If I'm out there: obsession and anxiety. If I'm not: obsession and melancholy. This year, I'm not.

Here's the roll call of where this race has found me:

- **2015** — Packing for Vegas and my very first Badwater.
- **2016** — Back in Vegas afterward, celebrating my finish (as one does, for a solid couple of days).
- 2017 — Recovering from my first surgery on *this very foot*, watching my friends out there after pulling out three weeks before the race.
- 2018 — On the course crewing my BFF, Leslie, who DNF'd at mile 50.
- **2019** — Back in Vegas again, celebrating my fastest time yet.
- 2020 — Cancelled, but in Vegas anyway; I'd already arrived to race before it was called off for COVID.
- 2021 — At the finish line, watching Leslie finally get it done.
- 2022 — Watching my friends out there while I recovered from this same procedure on my left foot.
- 2023 — Watching my friends out there, telling myself I didn't miss it. (I did.)
- 2024 — Back on the course crewing Leslie, who finished for the second time.
- 2025 — Watching my friends out there, telling myself I didn't miss it. (Still lying.)
- 2026 — Getting ready to obsessively refresh the tracker, wishing with everything I've got that I was out there.

In 2016, one of my crew, Bob, put it better than I ever could: "It doesn't matter if you're running it or crewing it. This race changes you."

It does. I can't explain what the finish line feels like, whether you got there yourself or someone you love did. Every single time, I've dissolved into tears. That's the kind of race it is.

It starts in five days, at 8pm, and the course stays open for 45 hours. But it's that first night I keep coming back to: the gun goes off, and then it's just you and the open desert. If you're even a little curious what that's like, watch [*Running on the Sun*](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dl3laLhbCsw), a documentary trailing a handful of runners through the 1999 race. Worth every minute.

So here I am again, parked in that tiny space between obsession and melancholy, vowing as always to get back out there. *One day.*

Words can't carry the rest of it. So I'll let the desert do the talking.
