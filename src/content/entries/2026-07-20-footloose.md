THE STITCHES CAME OUT!!! Hooooooooraaaaaaaaaaayyyyyyyy... one more step (har-har) towards recovery. We are 3 weeks in, and today [Dr. Stein](/entries/2026-06-17-doctors-orders) popped his head in and was really blown away by how good the foot looks. When the surgeon is excited, that's a *very good thing*.

Want to see what it looks like? Here's <a href="#past-the-photos">your chance to bail out</a> if you're squeamish, but I promise, it looks less franken-footy than it did last week.

Ok, now that the pics are out of the way, here's where we are. I will return in 3 weeks, on the 13th of August, to get the [shish-k-bob out of my toe](/entries/2026-07-10-under-pressure), take full x-rays, and, fingers crossed, get the thumbs up to go to partial weight bearing. If things continue to improve at the current rate, that's highly likely. Last time we had to go to 8 weeks, but the foot wasn't doing quite as well that go-round. I'm guessing the cleaner approach and tight recovery protocol have been helping a lot, as have [my peptides](/entries/2026-06-24-stuck-on-you), which research says can definitely speed healing.

Anyway, this is all good. I was also told to wash [the shin](/entries/2026-07-16-itchycoo-park) and wrap it in a different material, because I'm getting some real pain from rubbing against the ace bandage when I'm on the [iWalk](https://iwalk-free.com/). To the point that it's red, inflamed, and itchy/patchy. So that's the next thing I need to watch. But the goal today was simply to get those little pesky threads out (and there were a LOT) and get the medical clearance to keep on keeping on.

Mission accomplished. Three weeks of holding this foot together, and those threads have officially been relieved of duty. The hardware stays a while longer, the shin gets a new wardrobe, and I get to spend the next three weeks doing exactly what I've been doing: elevating, healing, and behaving myself. But today, at least on paper, this foot is officially cut loose.
