## The iWALK is part of the program

<img class="float-right xs" src="/entries/iwalker.png" alt="A smiling woman in a gray t-shirt and blue jeans throws both arms up in the air, hands free, with a black-and-blue iWALK hands-free crutch strapped to her bent left leg and a blue sneaker on her standing right foot." />

It looks like a gadget, but it's doing real PT work. Living on crutches 24/7 dumps the whole load onto my hips, lower back, and one good leg, and that's how you trade a foot problem for a hip problem. The [iWALK hands-free crutch](https://www.amazon.com/iWALK3-0-Hands-Free-Crutch-Alternative/dp/B08WJRWR57) straps to my bent knee and lets me stand and move upright, evenly, with both hands back. It keeps me out of the baby-giraffe crutch hunch and keeps my hips from getting totally jacked up while the foot heals. [The full gear-day story is in the journal](/entries/2026-06-21-man-in-motion).
